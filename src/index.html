Kibera


Kibera is a vast, informal settlement in Nairobi, Kenya. It is home to an estimated 1.7 million people, making it the largest slum in Africa. Kibera is a densely populated area with poor infrastructure and limited access to basic services. Residents of Kibera face a number of challenges, including poverty, unemployment, crime, and disease. Despite these challenges, Kibera is a vibrant community with a strong sense of solidarity. Residents of Kibera have made significant progress in improving their living conditions, and they continue to work hard to build a better future for themselves and their children.

Kibera Basic Services




Access to clean water is a major challenge in Kibera. The majority of residents rely on water vendors for their water supply. Water is often contaminated, and residents are at risk of waterborne diseases such as cholera and typhoid.


Sanitation is also a major challenge in Kibera. There are few public toilets, and many residents use open defecation. This practice contributes to the spread of disease.


Kibera Public Facilities


Access to education is also a challenge in Kibera. There are few schools, and many children do not attend school. This is due to a number of factors, including poverty, lack of space, and language barriers.
